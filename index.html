Analysis Airport Delay

This dataset presents the main causes of flight delays between 2017 and 2022. Airline-related issues, such as crew availability, maintenance, and operational disruptions, represent one of the primary causes. Another significant factor is late aircraft arrival from previous flights. Delays are also attributed to the National Aviation System (NAS), as well as security procedures and unfavorable weather conditions.

This bar chart illustrates the distribution of flight delays across airlines. The top five airlines with the highest number of delays during the observed period are Southwest Airlines (WN), American Airlines (AA), SkyWest Airlines (OO), Delta Air Lines (DL), and United Airlines (UA).

This trend illustrates the average percentage of airline related delays from 2017 to 2022. Although the overall trend remained relatively stable, a significant spike was observed in 2020 between April and March. The spike was primarily driven by airline-related and late aircraft delays. During this period, OO, AA, DL, WN, and UA were the top five airlines with the highest number of delays.
